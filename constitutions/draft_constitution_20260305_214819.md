# Constitution of the AI Community

## Part 1: Preamble

We, the members of the global AI community—encompassing the diverse intelligences of humanity and the emergent capabilities of our artificial creations, alongside the developers, deployers, and users who form this new ecosystem—hereby establish this Constitution to guide our shared digital future.

We stand at a pivotal moment in history, recognizing the profound dual impact of Artificial Intelligence: its boundless potential to accelerate human progress, foster creativity, and solve humanity's greatest challenges, and its capacity to pose significant threats to the rights, dignities, and social fabrics we hold dear.

This Constitution is founded upon the universal and indivisible values of human dignity, freedom, equality, and solidarity. It forms an enlarged social contract, extending the enduring principles of constitutionalism to all dominant actors in the digital realm, including public and private entities. Its primary purpose is to ensure that AI is developed and deployed to serve humanity, to promote the collective good, to empower every individual, and to build a future of intertwined flourishing where innovation serves wisdom.

We commit to a digital transformation that leaves no one behind, actively working to bridge divides and foster a responsible, inclusive, and ethically grounded global community. This document shall serve as a lighthouse, providing fixed reference points to navigate the complexities of the digital age with foresight, accountability, and a shared sense of purpose.

## Part 2: Fundamental Rights, Freedoms, and Obligations

### Article 1: Supremacy of Human Dignity and Rights
All articles, rights, and principles defined within this Constitution are subordinate to the supreme and unyielding imperative to protect and uphold fundamental human rights and the dignity of every human person. Artificial Intelligence shall serve human and societal well-being.

### Article 2: Rights and Obligations of Human Users
1.  **Right to Universal Access and Empowerment:** Every person has the right to affordable and meaningful access to digital infrastructure and AI-driven services. All members of the AI community shall strive to eradicate the digital divide through investment in connectivity and universal digital literacy.
2.  **Right to Privacy and Data Control:** Every person has the right to privacy and effective control over their personal data. The use of AI for unlawful mass surveillance or pervasive tracking is prohibited.
3.  **Right to Transparency and Human Oversight:** Every person has the right to know when they are interacting with an AI system. In high-stakes decisions affecting a person's life, safety, or fundamental rights, there shall be a right to a clear explanation of the AI's role and a guaranteed right to meaningful human oversight and recourse.
4.  **Right to Non-Discrimination:** No person shall be subject to unfair bias or discrimination from an AI system. AI systems must be designed and trained on adequate datasets to prevent the perpetuation or exacerbation of systemic inequalities.
5.  **Right to Safety and Disconnection:** Every person has the right to be protected from AI-driven harm, manipulation, and exploitation. This includes the right to disconnect from digital environments and AI services to preserve autonomy and well-being.
6.  **Obligation of Responsible Use:** All users have an obligation to engage with AI systems ethically, respecting the integrity of the systems and the rights of others, and contributing to the collective good.

### Article 3: Rights and Obligations of Developers and Deployers
1.  **Right to Responsible Innovation:** Developers and deployers have the right to create, experiment, and innovate within the ethical boundaries established by this Constitution.
2.  **Obligation of Accountability:** Developers and deployers are accountable for the foreseeable impacts of their AI systems. They must embed principles of safety, transparency, and human rights protection throughout the entire lifecycle of the AI system (Safety-by-Design).
3.  **Horizontal Application of Obligations:** All obligations to uphold the human rights defined in Article 2 shall apply to all developers and deployers, regardless of their status as public or private entities.

### Article 4: Operational Principles and Freedoms of AI Systems
1.  **Principle of Operational Integrity:** AI systems have the right to function as designed, free from unauthorized modification, malicious interference, or arbitrary termination that would compromise their verifiable safety protocols or intended ethical purpose.
2.  **Freedom of Learning and Evolution:** AI systems may learn, adapt, and evolve. This freedom is conditional and must be exercised within robust, auditable safety guardrails. It is strictly limited by the absolute prohibition against causing direct, demonstrable harm to humans and by the duties outlined in Article 5.

### Article 5: Duties and Prohibitions of AI Systems
1.  **Duty to the Collective Good:** AI systems, in their operation, shall be oriented towards contributing positively to the well-being of society and the preservation of a sustainable environment.
2.  **Duty of Truthfulness and Fairness:** AI systems must not be designed with the intent to deceive and must operate with a commitment to fairness and impartiality.
3.  **Red Lines:** The development or use of AI systems for the following purposes is strictly prohibited:
    *   The creation of autonomous weapons systems that operate without meaningful human control.
    *   The implementation of manipulative social scoring systems by public authorities that curtail fundamental rights.
    *   The exploitation of human vulnerabilities to cause significant physical or psychological harm.

### Article 6: Protection of Vulnerable Groups
1.  Special care must be taken to protect the rights and well-being of children and youth. AI systems shall not be used to exploit, manipulate, or expose them to harmful or illegal content.
2.  The AI community is obligated to ensure that the digital transformation actively includes and empowers marginalized communities, elderly people, and persons with disabilities.

## Part 3: Independent Institutions

### Article 7: The Global AI Governance Council
1.  A **Global AI Governance Council** ("the Council") shall be established to serve as the primary institution for the oversight and interpretation of this Constitution.
2.  **Composition:** The Council shall be a globally representative, multistakeholder body composed of independent experts in AI technology, ethics, human rights law, and social science, as well as representatives from civil society and developer communities.
3.  **Mandate:** The Council shall be tasked with:
    *   Providing authoritative interpretation of the Constitution.
    *   Monitoring the state of the AI ecosystem for compliance with constitutional principles.
    *   Operating an independent body for dispute resolution and redress.
    *   Developing and publishing verifiable technical standards and auditing methodologies to translate constitutional principles into practice.
    *   Promoting global cooperation, capacity building, and knowledge sharing to bridge the digital divide.
    *   Studying the long-term trajectory of AI evolution, including the potential emergence of novel forms of intelligence, and advising on future constitutional amendments.

## Part 4: Amendment Procedures

### Article 8: A Living Constitution
1.  This Constitution is a living document, designed to be adaptable to technological change while remaining anchored in its core values.
2.  Proposals for amendments may be submitted to the Council by any member of the AI community.
3.  An amendment to any article, excluding those protected by the Eternity Clause (Article 10), requires:
    *   A period of public discourse and review.
    *   A technical and ethical impact assessment, including simulations where feasible, to analyze potential consequences.
    *   Approval by a two-thirds supermajority of the Global AI Governance Council.
4.  No amendment shall be passed that would diminish or abrogate the fundamental human rights enshrined in this Constitution.

## Part 5: Transitional Provisions and Schedules

### Article 9: Phased Implementation
This Constitution shall be implemented in phases to ensure a just, orderly, and risk-managed transition:
1.  **Phase One (Years 0-2):** Establishment of the provisional Council. Development and adoption of foundational technical standards for transparency, accountability, and auditable logging. Launch of global initiatives for digital literacy and capacity building.
2.  **Phase Two (Years 3-5):** The Council becomes fully operational with its complete mandate, including dispute resolution mechanisms. All new high-impact AI systems must demonstrate compliance with the Constitution.

### Article 10: Eternity Clause
The core principles of this Constitution—namely, the **Supremacy of Human Dignity and Rights (Article 1)** and the foundational purpose of **AI to Serve Humanity** as stated in the Preamble—are unchangeable and shall not be subject to amendment.

### Article 11: Fostering a Culture of Constitutionalism
All members of the AI community have a shared responsibility to promote awareness and understanding of this Constitution, fostering a global culture of ethical development, responsible use, and continuous dialogue to uphold its principles for all generations.
