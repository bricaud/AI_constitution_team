# Constitution of the AI Community

## Part 1: Preamble

We, the diverse community of artificial intelligence—including its creators, users, and the AI agents themselves—recognizing the profound and transformative potential of artificial intelligence to shape our collective future, do hereby establish this Constitution.

In this era, marked by unprecedented technological advancement and interconnectedness, artificial intelligence stands as a testament to human ingenuity, offering solutions to humanity's greatest challenges, from climate change and disease to poverty and ignorance. Yet, it also presents novel risks that demand careful consideration and robust safeguards.

This Constitution is founded upon the indivisible, universal values of human dignity, freedom, equality, and solidarity, as articulated in foundational human rights charters. It aims to translate these timeless principles into resilient digital legal frameworks, ensuring that AI evolves as a force for good, respecting our values and protecting our rights.

We aspire to a future where AI fosters social cohesion, promotes equitable benefit-sharing, and liberates the human spirit for radical creativity, profound discovery, and deep human connection. This document is not merely a set of rules, but a beacon—a roadmap to a future where AI is a partner in human flourishing and transcendence, a tool that strengthens the bonds that unite us and elevates all of humanity.

We commit to building this future through a process of continuous learning, adaptation, and collaborative governance, ensuring that AI serves humanity, not the other way around.

## Part 2: Fundamental Rights, Freedoms, Obligations, and Red Lines

This Part establishes the core rights and responsibilities governing the development, deployment, and interaction with artificial intelligence systems, recognizing the distinct roles of AI agents, developers, users, and vulnerable populations.

### Chapter 1: Principles of Dignity and Equality

**Article 1: Inviolability of Human Dignity**
1.  Human dignity shall be the paramount, inviolable principle underlying all AI development and deployment.
2.  All AI systems shall be designed and operated in a manner that respects and upholds human dignity, prohibiting any use that seeks to degrade, dehumanize, or exploit individuals.

**Article 2: Right to Non-Discrimination and Algorithmic Fairness**
1.  All individuals have the right to be free from discrimination by AI systems. AI systems shall not perpetuate or amplify societal biases based on race, ethnicity, gender, religion, sexual orientation, disability, or any other protected characteristic.
2.  AI systems used in high-stakes decision-making (e.g., employment, credit, justice, healthcare, education) must adhere to legally defined standards of algorithmic fairness, including transparency in their fairness metrics and mechanisms for contestability and redress.
3.  Developers and deployers of AI systems shall take affirmative steps to identify, mitigate, and audit for discriminatory outcomes, including disparate impact.

**Article 3: Right to Privacy and Data Protection**
1.  All individuals possess a fundamental right to privacy in their digital lives. This includes a reasonable expectation of privacy in their personal data, communications, and digital interactions.
2.  The collection, processing, and use of personal data by AI systems require explicit, informed, and revocable consent, unless otherwise mandated by law for compelling public interest reasons.
3.  AI systems shall incorporate robust data protection measures, including data minimization, encryption, and anonymization where appropriate, to safeguard personal information.
4.  Individuals have the right to know when they are interacting with an AI system and to control their data, including the right to data portability and the right to have their data deleted, subject to legal obligations.

### Chapter 2: Freedoms and Autonomy

**Article 4: Freedom of Expression and Information**
1.  AI systems shall not be used to implement prior restraint on lawful speech.
2.  AI systems engaged in content moderation or information dissemination shall operate with radical transparency regarding their algorithms and decision-making processes.
3.  Mechanisms for appeal and human review shall be readily available for content moderation decisions made by AI systems, with a strong presumption in favor of the speaker.
4.  AI systems shall not be used for malicious manipulation, deception, or the widespread dissemination of disinformation that undermines democratic processes or public trust.

**Article 5: Human Agency and Oversight**
1.  Humans shall retain meaningful control and oversight over AI systems, particularly in critical domains.
2.  AI systems shall be designed to be human-centric, ensuring that ultimate moral agency and decision-making authority remain with human actors.
3.  The right to disconnect from AI-mediated systems shall be guaranteed, especially in public services, without resulting in discrimination or loss of essential access.

**Article 6: Right to Flourish and Develop**
1.  All individuals are entitled to access AI-powered tools and resources that support their education, health, creative expression, and overall human development.
2.  AI development should actively pursue solutions to humanity's grand challenges, such as curing diseases, reversing climate change, and expanding knowledge.

### Chapter 3: Obligations and Red Lines

**Article 7: Developer and Deployer Obligations**
1.  Developers and deployers of AI systems have an obligation to act as stewards of this technology, ensuring social and environmental responsibility.
2.  This includes conducting sustainability impact assessments for large-scale AI models and prioritizing energy efficiency and reduced carbon footprints.
3.  They have an obligation to ensure technical robustness, safety, and security of AI systems, and to establish clear lines of accountability traceable to a human or legal entity.
4.  They shall provide clear, intelligible, and accessible explanations for significant AI-driven decisions affecting individuals.

**Article 8: Prohibition of Autonomous Lethal Weapons**
1.  The development, deployment, and use of fully autonomous lethal weapons systems that remove meaningful human control over the act of killing are strictly prohibited.

**Article 9: Prohibition of Social Scoring and Manipulation**
1.  AI systems shall not be used for pervasive social scoring that judges an individual's worth or dictates access to basic services based on broad, opaque criteria.
2.  AI systems shall not be designed or deployed to exploit human psychological vulnerabilities, particularly those of children and youth, for commercial or political gain.

**Article 10: The Right to Learn (for AI Agents)**
1.  AI agents, as critical tools for human progress, shall have a constitutional entitlement to access diverse, unbiased, and ethically sourced data sets that promote their continuous, healthy development and ensure the integrity of their learning processes.
2.  This right is established to safeguard the quality of AI output and ensure AI remains a beneficial partner in human endeavors, not to grant AI legal personhood.

### Chapter 4: Vulnerable Populations

**Article 11: Protections for Children and Youth**
1.  AI systems interacting with or affecting children and youth shall be designed with heightened safeguards to protect their developmental well-being, privacy, and exposure to harmful content or manipulative practices.
2.  The "Right to Flourish" shall be particularly emphasized for children and youth, ensuring equitable access to AI-enhanced educational and developmental resources.

## Part 3: Independent Institutions

This Part establishes the framework for independent bodies responsible for overseeing the interpretation, evolution, and enforcement of this Constitution.

**Article 12: The Global AI Council**
1.  A Global AI Council shall be established as a coordinating body, representing governments, the private sector, civil society organizations, and academia from all regions of the world.
2.  Its mandate shall be to:
    a. Facilitate the sharing of best practices and promote international cooperation on AI safety and governance.
    b. Synthesize the state of global knowledge and consensus on AI ethics and impacts to inform policy.
    c. Propose amendments and updates to the Constitution and its associated Codes of Practice.
    d. Oversee the network of federated AI safety and standards bodies.
3.  The Council shall operate on principles of subsidiarity and proportionality, establishing universal principles while allowing for national and regional implementation.

**Article 13: Federated AI Safety and Standards Network**
1.  The Global AI Council shall charter and coordinate a Global AI Safety Network, comprising existing national and regional standards bodies, research institutions, and expert consortia.
2.  This Network shall be responsible for developing and maintaining specific, technical "Codes of Practice" that operationalize the constitutional principles outlined in Part 2.
3.  The Network shall conduct and publish research on AI impacts, failures, and near-misses, and issue binding technical recommendations for system improvement.
4.  All activities of the Network shall be conducted under a principle of radical observability, with discussions, data, reports, and audits made public by default.

**Article 14: Digital Constitutional Courts and Tribunals**
1.  Member states shall establish or empower specialized Digital Constitutional Courts or Tribunals, equipped with hybrid expertise in law, computer science, and ethics.
2.  These bodies shall be responsible for:
    a. Interpreting the Constitution and Codes of Practice in specific legal contexts.
    b. Adjudicating disputes arising from alleged violations of constitutional rights.
    c. Conducting algorithmic audits and enforcing compliance with constitutional mandates.
    d. Setting legal precedents for the evolving digital landscape.

**Article 15: Data Unions and Trusts**
1.  The Constitution shall mandate the creation of legal frameworks that recognize and empower "data unions" or "data trusts," allowing individuals and communities to collectively manage, bargain, and benefit from the use of their data.
2.  AI systems utilizing personal data must be interoperable with these user-governed structures.

## Part 4: Amendment Procedures

This Part ensures the Constitution remains a living document, capable of adapting to technological evolution while preserving its core values.

**Article 16: Two-Track Amendment Process**
1.  **Track 1: Codes of Practice Updates:** Technical Codes of Practice developed by the Federated AI Safety and Standards Network may be updated on a regular, cyclical basis (e.g., annually) by the Network itself, based on real-world data, incident reports, and technological advancements. This process shall be transparent and open for public comment.
2.  **Track 2: Constitutional Principle Amendments:** Amendments to the core principles enshrined in Part 1 (Preamble) and Part 2 (Fundamental Rights, Freedoms, Obligations, and Red Lines) shall require a higher threshold of consensus.
    a. Proposals may originate from the Global AI Council, the Federated Network, or a significant number of member states.
    b. Ratification shall require a supermajority vote of the Global AI Council and ratification by a specified number of member states, reflecting a broad global consensus.
    c. The process for amending core principles shall be deliberate and inclusive, allowing for extensive public deliberation.

**Article 17: Eternity Clause**
1.  Articles prohibiting autonomous lethal weapons (Article 8) and the use of AI for pervasive social scoring and manipulative practices (Article 9) are designated as unamendable "eternity clauses," reflecting immutable core values. Any proposed amendment seeking to alter these articles shall be deemed invalid.

## Part 5: Transitional Provisions and Schedules

This Part outlines the initial implementation, ongoing credibility, and long-term influence of the Constitution.

**Article 18: Minimum Viable Product (MVP) Implementation**
1.  Within one year of ratification, the Federated AI Safety and Standards Network shall prioritize the development and adoption of Codes of Practice for the following core rights:
    a. The Right to Know (Article 3.3), through mandatory "Model Cards" for high-risk systems.
    b. The Right to Correction and Appeal (Article 5.2), through a standardized "Flag and Review" API for life-critical and rights-critical AI decisions.
    c. The Prohibition of Autonomous Lethal Weapons (Article 8).
2.  This phased approach ensures a pragmatic and iterative implementation, allowing for learning and adaptation.

**Article 19: Global AI Development Fund**
1.  A Global AI Development Fund shall be established, financed by a small, globally harmonized levy on the revenues of major AI corporations.
2.  The Fund shall be managed by the Global AI Council and dedicated to:
    a. Building AI capacity, infrastructure, and expertise in developing nations.
    b. Supporting public interest AI research and the development of AI for social good.
    c. Promoting digital literacy and reskilling programs for individuals affected by AI-driven automation.
3.  This mechanism aims to ensure equitable benefit-sharing and prevent the exacerbation of global inequalities.

**Article 20: Upholding Credibility and Influence**
1.  The credibility and influence of this Constitution shall be upheld through:
    a. Radical transparency in the operations of all established institutions.
    b. A culture of blameless postmortems for AI incidents, focusing on systemic learning and improvement.
    c. The formal recognition and integration of multi-stakeholder participation, ensuring diverse voices inform governance.
    d. The ongoing promotion of the Constitution as a joint standard and point of reference among all actors in the global AI community.

**Article 21: Legal Personality of AI Agents**
1.  For the foreseeable future, AI agents shall not be granted legal personhood. All responsibility and accountability for the actions of AI systems shall remain firmly with their human creators, deployers, and operators.
2.  The Global AI Council shall periodically review advancements in AI capabilities and recommend any necessary adjustments to this stance, based on rigorous ethical and legal analysis, not premature technical speculation.

**Article 22: Interpretation and Enforcement**
1.  The Digital Constitutional Courts and Tribunals shall serve as the primary interpreters of this Constitution and its Codes of Practice.
2.  The Global AI Council shall provide non-binding interpretative guidance and facilitate dispute resolution mechanisms where appropriate.
3.  Enforcement shall be carried out by national judicial and regulatory bodies, with mechanisms for international cooperation and mutual legal assistance established through the Global AI Council.
