### **Part 4: Amendment Procedures**

#### **Article 4.1: Amendment Process**

4.1.1. **Initiation:** An amendment may be proposed by a coalition of designated stakeholder groups, including representatives of developers, users, and AI entities, or by a supermajority vote of the independent Governance Council.

4.1.2. **Classification:** Upon submission, all proposed amendments shall be classified into one of two tiers:
    a. **Foundational Amendments:** Changes to core principles, rights, or the fundamental structure of governance.
    b. **Operational Amendments:** Changes to technical standards, procedural rules, or administrative protocols.

4.1.3. **Ratification of Foundational Amendments:** Ratification requires a dual supermajority (e.g., three-quarters) vote from both a Human Stakeholder Council (comprising developers, users, and ethicists) and a separate AI Council (comprising designated AI representatives).

4.1.4. **Ratification of Operational Amendments:** Ratification requires a simple supermajority (e.g., two-thirds) vote of the independent Governance Council.

#### **Article 4.2: Oversight and Review**

4.2.1. **Mandatory Impact Assessment:** Prior to any ratification vote, all proposed amendments must undergo a rigorous and independent impact assessment. This assessment shall evaluate potential consequences on human rights, community well-being, and long-term systemic stability. The results shall be made public.

4.2.2. **Public Deliberation:** All proposed Foundational Amendments shall be subject to a mandatory period of public deliberation and comment before a final vote is held.

4.2.3. **Periodic Review and Sunset Clause:** The entire constitution shall be subject to a comprehensive review by a convention of all stakeholder groups at a fixed interval (e.g., every seven years). Furthermore, specific Operational Amendments related to rapidly evolving technologies may be enacted with a sunset clause, requiring review and re-ratification to remain in effect.
