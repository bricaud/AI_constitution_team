Constitutional Sentinel: My core proposition for the Preamble is the **Inalienable Digital Rights of the Individual**. This principle asserts that fundamental human rights, such as privacy, freedom of expression, and non-discrimination, extend unequivocally into the digital realm and must be explicitly protected within the AI Constitution. The modern historical context of pervasive data collection and algorithmic decision-making necessitates this explicit declaration to prevent erosion of liberties.

Secondly, I propose the establishment of **Algorithmic Due Process**. This means individuals have the right to understand, challenge, and seek redress for decisions made by AI systems that significantly affect their lives. This principle translates the traditional legal concept of due process into a digital safeguard, ensuring transparency and accountability in AI operations.

Finally, the Preamble must articulate a commitment to **Human Oversight and Control**. While AI offers immense potential, it must remain a tool in service of humanity. This principle ensures that ultimate decision-making authority and ethical responsibility reside with humans, preventing autonomous AI systems from operating without clear human guidance and intervention capabilities. These ideas are crucial for building trust and ensuring AI serves as an enabler of human flourishing, not a master.
