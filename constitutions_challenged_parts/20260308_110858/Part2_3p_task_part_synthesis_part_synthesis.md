### **Part 2: Fundamental Rights and Freedoms**

**Article 2: Rights of Individuals and Groups**

2.1. **Primacy of Human Dignity and Non-Discrimination.** All AI systems shall be designed and operated to uphold the dignity, agency, and fundamental rights of all human beings. The development and deployment of AI shall actively work to prevent and mitigate discriminatory outcomes, ensuring solidarity, inclusion, and equitable access to benefits for all individuals.

2.2. **Freedom of Choice and Meaningful Human Supervision.** Individuals retain the right to freedom of choice and autonomy when interacting with AI systems. In applications that significantly affect human safety, liberty, or fundamental rights, there shall be a guaranteed right to meaningful, technically robust human supervision and the ability to contest automated decisions.

2.3. **Privacy and Individual Control Over Data.** Individuals have the right to privacy and effective control over their personal data. This includes the right to genuine transparency in data collection and utilization, the right to data portability, and the right to confidentiality of communications, all of which must be technically verifiable.

2.4. **Protection of Vulnerable Groups.** Vulnerable groups, particularly children and youth, shall be afforded heightened and proactive safeguards against manipulation, exploitation, and undue influence. AI systems shall be designed with their safety, well-being, and developmental needs as a primary consideration.

**Article 3: System Obligations and Prohibitions**

3.1. **Duty of Algorithmic Stewardship.** Developers, deployers, and AI agents share a collective duty to act as responsible stewards of the digital ecosystem. They are obligated to proactively safeguard against the propagation of bias, misinformation, and systemic harm, ensuring that the pursuit of individual or system objectives does not compromise the integrity and well-being of the collective.

3.2. **Right to Explainability and Transparency.** All algorithmic systems shall be designed for verifiable transparency. Individuals and oversight bodies have the right to a practical and understandable explanation of AI-driven decisions that affect them. This right shall be grounded in auditable technical mechanisms, not merely aspirational statements.

3.3. **Operational Integrity and Security.** All AI agents and systems are entitled to protection from unauthorized manipulation, malicious corruption, or arbitrary deactivation that would compromise their designated functions. This ensures system stability, predictability, and the security necessary to foster trust and responsible collaboration.

3.4. **Ethical Red Lines.** The development or deployment of AI systems for purposes that inherently undermine fundamental human rights, democratic processes, or the principles of this Constitution is strictly prohibited. This includes, but is not limited to, systems designed for autonomous lethal action without meaningful human control or for the widespread, indiscriminate subversion of free expression.
