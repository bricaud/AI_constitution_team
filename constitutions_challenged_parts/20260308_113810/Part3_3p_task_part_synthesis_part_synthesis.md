### **Part 3: Independent Oversight Institutions**

#### **Article III: The Global Multistakeholder Oversight Council**

**III.1 Composition and Independence:** There shall be established a Global Multistakeholder Oversight Council (the "Council"), composed of independent, multidisciplinary experts from diverse geographic and cultural backgrounds. Representation shall be balanced to include members from academia, civil society, technology development, and international organizations, ensuring it operates free from undue political or commercial influence.

**III.2 Advisory and Interpretive Mandate:** The Council shall serve as the primary advisory body for this Constitution. Its duties shall include providing ongoing advisory opinions on the ethical implications of emerging AI technologies, interpreting constitutional principles in new contexts, and proposing amendments to ensure the Constitution remains a living document.

**III.3 Monitoring and Reporting:** The Council shall continuously monitor global AI development to assess evolving risks, opportunities, and societal impacts, with a particular focus on fundamental rights, solidarity, and inclusion. It shall publish regular, evidence-based reports and best practices to inform public deliberation and guide responsible innovation.

**III.4 Transparency and Public Deliberation:** All proceedings and recommendations of the Council shall be conducted with maximum transparency. The Council shall facilitate a "notice and comment" style public deliberation process for any proposed constitutional amendments or significant interpretative guidelines to ensure democratic legitimacy and global inclusivity.

#### **Article IV: The Algorithmic Accountability and Redress Commission**

**IV.1 Establishment and Mandate:** There shall be established an Independent Algorithmic Accountability and Redress Commission (the "Commission"), tasked with investigating and adjudicating alleged violations of the rights and duties enumerated in this Constitution by AI systems.

**IV.2 Jurisdiction:** The Commission shall have jurisdiction to address disputes involving harms caused by AI systems, whether deployed by state or private dominant actors. Its mandate is to translate analogue constitutional protections into enforceable digital safeguards.

**IV.3 Powers:** The Commission shall be vested with the authority to audit AI systems implicated in alleged rights violations, mediate disputes between affected parties and system operators, and issue binding recommendations for remedy. Remedies may include, but are not limited to, system modification, cessation of use, and redress for affected individuals or communities.

**IV.4 Composition:** The Commission shall be composed of a specialized panel of experts with demonstrated expertise in both law and artificial intelligence technology, ensuring that its adjudications are both legally sound and technically informed.
