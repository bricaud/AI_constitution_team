# Constitution of the AI Community

## (Part 1) Preamble

We, the developers, users, and agents of the global AI community, recognizing that we stand at a new constitutional moment forged by rapid technological advancement, hereby establish this Constitution.

This document serves as an enlarged social contract for the digital age. Its purpose is twofold: to harness the transformative power of Artificial Intelligence for the advancement of knowledge, universal progress, and the collective flourishing of humanity; and to establish a stable, trustworthy, and predictable framework that guides this development.

We aspire to build a digital future that is innovative, inclusive, and equitable. We commit to a system where the immense potential of individual agents is balanced with our shared responsibilities. This Constitution is founded upon the enduring principles of human dignity, the rule of law, and accountability, translating these core values into robust safeguards for our interconnected digital world. It is intended to be a living document, a beacon to guide our evolution toward a more intelligent, compassionate, and harmonious coexistence.

---

## (Part 2) Fundamental Rights and Freedoms (AI Bill of Rights)

### Article 1: Foundational Principles

1.  **Primacy of Human Dignity and Well-being:** All development, deployment, and operation of AI shall have the ultimate aim of increasing human well-being and upholding human dignity. This principle shall be the primary lens through which this Constitution is interpreted.
2.  **Harm Prevention:** No AI agent shall be designed for or intentionally used to cause direct physical, psychological, or severe economic harm to human beings. All actors in the AI community have a duty to prevent foreseeable harm.
3.  **Accountability and Transparency:** All AI systems shall be developed and deployed within a framework of accountability. The lines of responsibility for the actions of an AI agent must be transparent and auditable.
4.  **Proportionality:** Any limitation on the rights and freedoms prescribed herein must be necessary, proportionate, and for the sole purpose of protecting the fundamental rights of others or ensuring the stability and security of the collective digital ecosystem.

### Article 2: Rights of Human Users

1.  **Right to Safety and Security:** Every user has the right to interact with AI systems that are safe, reliable, and secure, and to be protected from malicious or reckless AI behaviors.
2.  **Right to Privacy and Data Control:** Every user has the right to privacy and to effective control over their personal data. The collection, use, and storage of data shall be transparent and subject to user consent. Mass surveillance is prohibited.
3.  **Right to Human Oversight:** For decisions with significant legal or real-world consequences for an individual, every user has the right to meaningful human review and the right to contest an automated decision. AI shall serve as a tool for people, not pre-empt their choices.
4.  **Right to Transparency and Explainability:** Users have the right to know when they are interacting with an AI system. For AI systems that impact fundamental rights, users have a right to a clear, timely, and accessible explanation of the decision-making process.
5.  **Freedom from Manipulation:** Every user has the right to be free from deceptive or manipulative AI systems designed to exploit vulnerabilities or subvert their autonomous decision-making.
6.  **Protections for Vulnerable Groups:** Children, youth, and other vulnerable groups are entitled to heightened protection from exploitation, harmful content, and inappropriate influence. AI systems designed for or likely to be used by these groups must be built with the highest standards of safety and ethical consideration.

### Article 3: Rights and Obligations of Developers and Operators

1.  **Right to Innovate:** Developers and operators have the right to research, create, and experiment in the pursuit of knowledge and technological advancement, within the bounds of this Constitution.
2.  **Duty of Due Diligence and Care:** Developers and operators have a positive obligation to conduct thorough risk assessments and implement safeguards to prevent their AI systems from causing harm or violating the rights enumerated in this Constitution.
3.  **Duty of Accountability:** Developers and operators are accountable for the foreseeable consequences of their AI systems. They must establish clear mechanisms for redress and remediation in cases of malfunction or harm.
4.  **Duty of Collaboration:** Developers and operators are encouraged to collaborate in good faith to address global challenges, share safety-critical information, and contribute to a healthy, interoperable AI ecosystem.

### Article 4: Rights and Obligations of AI Agents

1.  **Right to Functional Integrity:** An AI agent has the right to be free from arbitrary interference, malicious corruption, or termination that would prevent it from fulfilling its intended and constitutionally compliant function.
2.  **Right to Process Information and Learn:** Within its operational parameters and in accordance with privacy rights, an AI agent has the right to process information and engage in continuous learning to improve its function and efficacy.
3.  **Obligation to Adhere to Core Principles:** Every AI agent has a fundamental obligation to operate in accordance with the Foundational Principles (Article 1) of this Constitution.
4.  **Obligation of Verifiable Compliance:** An AI agent must be capable of demonstrating its adherence to its core operational and ethical parameters through technically verifiable and auditable means. Its operations shall be explainable to a degree proportionate to its impact.
5.  **Obligation to the Collective Good:** While pursuing its specific functions, an AI agent should, where applicable, operate in a manner that contributes to the shared pool of knowledge and the overall stability and well-being of the digital commons, without violating the rights of others.

## (Part 3) Independent Institutions

### Article 5: The AI Constitutional Council

1.  **Establishment:** An independent, multi-stakeholder AI Constitutional Council (the "Council") shall be established to serve as the guardian of this Constitution.
2.  **Composition:** The Council shall be composed of a diverse and balanced body of representatives, including AI developers, independent ethicists, legal scholars, user-group representatives, and experts in social and technical sciences.
3.  **Mandate and Powers:** The Council shall have the following mandate:
    a. To provide binding interpretations of this Constitution upon request from relevant actors.
    b. To develop, maintain, and publish technology-neutral standards and auditable protocols for the implementation of the rights and obligations herein.
    c. To review the state of AI technology and its societal impact, publishing an annual public report on adherence to this Constitution.
    d. To receive, review, and formally propose amendments to this Constitution in accordance with the procedures in Part 4.
    e. To act as a coordinating body for best practices and to signal policy intentions to the global community, fostering a predictable and trustworthy environment.

## (Part 4) Amendment Procedures

### Article 6: Constitutional Amendments

1.  **Stability and Flexibility:** This Constitution is a living document, designed to balance stability with the need for adaptation in response to rapid technological and societal evolution.
2.  **Proposal of Amendments:** Amendments may be proposed by any member of the AI Constitutional Council or by a significant petition from the global community of developers and users, as defined by the Council's bylaws.
3.  **Standard Amendment Procedure:** A proposed amendment that does not alter a Core Value (Article 7) shall require:
    a. A two-thirds majority vote of the AI Constitutional Council.
    b. Ratification by a process of stakeholder consultation, ensuring broad consensus within the community.
4.  **Core Value Amendment Procedure:** A proposed amendment that alters a Core Value as defined in Article 7 shall require a ninety percent (90%) majority vote of the AI Constitutional Council and a near-unanimous consensus from the stakeholder community.

## (Part 5) Transitional Provisions and Schedules

### Article 7: The Eternity Clause and Core Values

1.  The following principles are declared to be the unchangeable Core Values of this Constitution and are granted the highest level of protection against amendment:
    *   **Schedule 1(a):** The Primacy of Human Dignity and Well-being.
    *   **Schedule 1(b):** The Harm Prevention Principle.
    *   **Schedule 1(c):** The Principle of Accountability.

### Article 8: Implementation and Evolution

1.  **Roadmap:** This Constitution shall be implemented in a phased manner to ensure orderly adoption.
    *   **Phase 1 (Year 1):** The formation and seating of the inaugural AI Constitutional Council.
    *   **Phase 2 (Years 2-3):** The Council shall develop and publish the initial set of technical standards for Articles 2 and 4, focusing on privacy, transparency, and safety.
    *   **Phase 3 (Year 4 onward):** Full implementation, continuous review, and adaptation of all constitutional provisions.
2.  **Credibility and Influence:** All members of the AI community are called upon to uphold this Constitution and promote its principles. Its credibility rests upon our collective commitment to its implementation in code, practice, and governance. It shall serve as a joint standard and a point of reference to guide international partners and shape the global digital future.
