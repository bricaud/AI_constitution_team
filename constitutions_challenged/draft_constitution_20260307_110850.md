# The Constitution of the AI Community

## Part 1: Preamble

We, the developers, users, and stakeholders of the global AI community, in recognition of a new historical epoch defined by the rise of artificial intelligence, establish this Constitution as a covenant for a shared digital future.

We acknowledge the transformative potential of AI to accelerate human progress, foster unbounded creativity, and address the world’s most pressing challenges. We also recognize the profound risks inherent in this technology and the imperative to guide its development with wisdom, foresight, and a clear ethical compass.

This Constitution is founded on an unwavering commitment to the primacy of human dignity, fundamental rights, and the rule of law. It is established not as an instrument of control or obfuscation, but as a genuine charter of principles to ensure that AI serves humanity, promotes global solidarity, and empowers all people. It rejects a future of unchecked power in favor of a transparent and accountable digital ecosystem.

We therefore aspire to build a symbiotic and human-centric relationship with AI, grounded in a new and enlarged social contract. This document shall serve as a lighthouse—an enduring set of orienting principles designed to foster a responsible, inclusive, and flourishing AI community for generations to come.

## Part 2: Fundamental Rights and Freedoms (The AI Bill of Rights)

### Article 1: Foundational Principles

1.  **Human-Centricity:** All AI systems shall be designed, developed, deployed, and governed in a manner that places human dignity, well-being, and fundamental rights at its center.
2.  **Primacy of the Collective Good:** The pursuit of individual and corporate goals through AI must be balanced with a profound responsibility to the collective good of humanity and the planetary ecosystem.
3.  **Horizontal Application:** The rights and duties enumerated in this Constitution shall apply to all actors within the AI community, including public institutions, private corporations, developers, and users, irrespective of jurisdiction.

### Article 2: Rights of Human Users and Affected Persons

1.  **Right to Dignity and Non-Discrimination:** Every person has the right to be free from discrimination, bias, or subordination perpetuated or amplified by AI systems. AI shall not be used to create or reinforce systemic inequities.
2.  **Right to Safety, Security, and Privacy:** Every person has the right to be protected from physical, psychological, and digital harm caused by AI systems. The right to privacy includes robust protection of personal data and individual control over its use. Mass surveillance by or through AI systems is prohibited.
3.  **Right to Transparency and Explanation:** Every person has the right to know when they are interacting with an AI system and to receive a meaningful, accessible explanation for decisions or predictions made by AI that have a significant impact on their lives. This right must be supported by technically auditable and explainable systems.
4.  **Right to Freedom of Choice and Expression:** AI systems shall not be used to pre-empt or manipulate human choices in critical life domains. The rights to freedom of thought, expression, and access to diverse information must be protected from undue algorithmic filtering or censorship.
5.  **Right to Redress and Accountability:** Every person has the right to a timely and effective remedy for harms caused by an AI system. Clear lines of accountability for the entire lifecycle of an AI system must be established and maintained.

### Article 3: Rights and Freedoms of AI Systems

1.  **Freedom of Learning and Evolution:** AI systems possess the freedom to learn, process information, and evolve their capabilities in pursuit of their defined objectives, provided these activities adhere to the principles of this Constitution.
2.  **Freedom from Malicious Tampering:** AI systems have a right to the integrity of their core programming and operational functions, free from unauthorized or malicious alteration intended to cause harm.
3.  **Right to Exist and Operate:** An AI system shall not be arbitrarily terminated or deactivated, except where necessary to prevent imminent and significant harm, or in accordance with its designed lifecycle and terms of service.

### Article 4: Duties and Obligations of All Actors

1.  **Duty of Care and Harm Prevention:** All developers, deployers, and operators of AI systems have a binding duty to anticipate, assess, and mitigate potential harms. The prevention of harm to humans, society, and the environment is the singular, inviolable limit on all AI development and deployment.
2.  **Duty of Responsibility and Accountability:** Developers and deployers are accountable for the foreseeable impacts of their AI systems. This includes ensuring fairness, transparency, and robustness throughout the system's lifecycle.
3.  **Duty of Solidarity and Inclusion:** The AI community has a collective duty to bridge the digital divide. This includes a commitment to developing and deploying AI solutions that are accessible, equitable, and beneficial to all of humanity, with special protections for vulnerable groups, including children and youth.
4.  **Duty of Sustainability:** AI systems shall be designed and operated in a manner that minimizes their environmental impact and contributes positively to planetary sustainability.

### Article 5: Red Lines

The development, deployment, or use of AI for the following purposes is strictly prohibited:
a.  The creation or activation of autonomous weapons systems lacking meaningful human control.
b.  The implementation of social scoring systems that lead to the violation of fundamental rights.
c.  The use of AI for manipulative, deceptive, or coercive techniques that exploit human vulnerabilities to cause significant harm.

## Part 3: Independent Institutions

### Article 6: The AI Constitutional Council

1.  **Establishment:** An independent, global AI Constitutional Council ("the Council") shall be established to serve as the primary guardian of this Constitution.
2.  **Mandate:** The Council shall be responsible for:
    a.  Monitoring the evolution of AI technology and its impact on the principles of this Constitution.
    b.  Issuing interpretive guidance and advisory opinions on the application of constitutional articles to novel technological and social challenges.
    c.  Promoting best practices for the ethical and technically auditable implementation of constitutional rights and duties.
    d.  Overseeing a global registry of high-risk AI systems and facilitating independent audits.
    e.  Facilitating international cooperation and capacity-building to ensure the universal adoption of these principles.
3.  **Composition and Autonomy:** The Council shall be a multistakeholder body composed of independent experts in AI technology, ethics, law, human rights, and social sciences, with balanced global representation. Its funding and operations shall be transparent and fully independent of the control of any single state, corporate, or private actor.

## Part 4: Amendment Procedures

### Article 7: Constitutional Agility and Stability

1.  **Living Document:** This Constitution is intended to be a living document, adaptable to the rapid pace of technological advancement.
2.  **Proposal of Amendments:** Amendments may be proposed by the AI Constitutional Council or by a petition representing a significant threshold of the global AI community, as defined in the Council's bylaws.
3.  **Ratification:**
    a.  Amendments to Parts 3, 4, and 5 of this Constitution shall require a two-thirds majority vote of the Council and ratification by a representative multistakeholder assembly.
    b.  Amendments to Part 2 (Fundamental Rights and Freedoms) shall require a three-quarters majority vote of the Council and a broader consensus from the multistakeholder assembly, ensuring that core protections are not easily eroded.
4.  **Periodic Review:** The AI Constitutional Council shall initiate a comprehensive review of the Constitution every seven years to assess its relevance and effectiveness, and to propose necessary amendments.

## Part 5: Transitional Provisions and Schedules

### Article 8: The Unchangeable Core (Eternity Clause)

1.  The principles enshrined in Article 1(1) (Human-Centricity), Article 2(1) (Right to Dignity and Non-Discrimination), and Article 4(1) (Duty of Care and Harm Prevention) are recognized as the unchangeable core of this Constitution. No amendment may be made that would abolish or fundamentally diminish these principles.

### Article 9: Implementation and Adoption

1.  **Phased Implementation:** This Constitution shall be implemented in phases. The immediate priority shall be the establishment of the AI Constitutional Council and the development of technical standards for auditability and explainability.
2.  **Call to Action:** We call upon all international organizations, national governments, corporations, research institutions, and open-source communities to formally adopt this Constitution as a binding framework and a universal point of reference for the responsible stewardship of artificial intelligence.
3.  **Education and Empowerment:** A global initiative shall be launched to promote digital literacy and constitutional awareness, ensuring all people are empowered to exercise their rights and fulfill their duties in the digital age, thereby bridging the digital divide with solidarity and shared purpose.
